Process

The first thing I did was research the competition. This includes finding out about the market, the audience and analyzing what I found. From that, I create a list of requirements and constraints for quick reference when I am designing. After that, the creative brief so that I have a clear understanding of what is expected. After all the documentation is out of the way, the next step is to create thumbnails (or brainstorming sketches). After I decided upon a few designs that I think is appropriate, I create wireframes to determine where the image and text go. Using the wireframes as I guide, I then explore the different ideas in InDesign to get a better idea of which one to go with.

More Detail about Project

The goal of this project is to redesign a magazine cover and selected spread in order to achieve a better aesthetic appeal. The objective is to make it more appealing to the audience and differentiate from existing competition. The primary audience is the professor and teaching assistant. The secondary audiences are people who enjoy travelling and commercial aviation because the focus of this magazine is on commercial aviation and airports. The age range is around 15 and up as there is a lot of text and more specific terminology.

The main concept behind the redesign is simplicity. The idea is inspired by the atmosphere of international airports such as Tokyo Narita, London Heathrow, etc. After some researching, I noticed that the only real competition to this magazine is Airliners Magazine and they have similar design to my current one before the redesign. Thus I researched airports instead as I feel the design for those places is more coherent with my magazine content.

The design principles I used were balance, proportion, contrast and unity while the elements consists of mostly line and colour. Throughout the design process, I decided to work with a column grid with 6 columns. In addition, I also reviewed around 300 images and created different roughs to decide on the ones that I will use. I also decided on white as my primary text colour while using san serif font for titles and serif for text.

My palette consists of mostly shades of blue, white and black. Blue is chosen because it reflects the idea of aviation. White and black is used for the text because it has the highest contrast against the background and creates better readability. The same reasons apply for the choice of black text on the feature story spread. Additionally, Din Pro was used for titles in order to create a simple feeling while Pescadero was used for the actual text for easier reading. As for the images, I tried to find something that is different and reflects my design style with angles. Furthermore, as the main feature of this issue is Boeing's triple seven, the aircraft that is primary used are Boeing 777. Additionally, the plane for the story spread is chosen because United airlines (A member of Star Alliance) is the first carrier to buy a Boeing 777 and it correlated with the content of the spread.

Overall, I think it is a successful solution to the project objective as the spreads are aesthetically appealing and interesting. Furthermore, it is different from its competitor and thus should work better in attracting the target audiences and get ahead of the competition.
