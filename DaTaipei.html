Da Taipei Business Card

Process - Different Explorations

Below are a few different exploration of Typography/layout that I did

Programs/Equipment used:

  • - Canon EOS Rebel T1i

  • - Illustrator

Process

I started off by listing out the constraints that I have to work with as well as the needs of my client on a piece of paper. I first decide on the design approach best suited for the stall and then work my way up from there. As I was on location, I had the opportunity to survey the area, get a good feel of the atmosphere and take photos for reference. The stall had two really beautiful lanterns which captivated me and I wanted to use it on the card. The font choice was made based on the how well do it reflect the stall. As it is a food stall not an elaborate restaurant, I purposely worked with typefaces which had more of “hand written” feel to it. In a food stall, the customer could see who is cooking and thus I feel that by using a more hand written feel typeface, it could give off a much more open feeling compared to a more minimal clean typeface.

The next step is to explore different layouts. I feel that imagining in one’s head is something but it is also generally different from when we actually create it. Therefore I created multiple versions so that I can get a better sense of what works better.

More Detail about Project

During my trip to Taiwan, I came across a food stall in Taichung that sparked my interest. The food was different as they made use of Chinese herbal medicine as part of their ingredients. I later learned that this is called Soya-mixed meat. As I started chatting with the owner, they mentioned that they needed a redesign for their business card so I jumped in as I find this an interesting challenge. The requirements were that it needs to clearly communicate what the stall is as well as providing contact information. The time frame I had was one day. Overall, this project allowed me to demonstrate my proficiency in design with a different language as well as getting me some free food.
